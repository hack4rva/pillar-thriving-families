# Targeted Problem Statements - Thriving Families

## 1) Expanding Youth Employment Pathways

Score: 23/32 — Needs work
Critical data gap: D3=1 (lowest data readiness score across all 12 targeted statements)

### Problem statement
How might we use technology to improve how Richmond youth find, prepare for, and access early employment opportunities — including summer jobs, internships, and job-shadowing experiences — so that the path from interest to employment feels clear, supported, and achievable, while respecting youth labor laws, parental consent requirements, staffing capacity, and transportation barriers?

### Context
Early work experiences help young people build skills, confidence, and long-term workforce attachment. For many Richmond youth — especially those without strong adult support networks — these opportunities provide critical exposure. The process of securing a first job is fragmented and confusing. Existing programs through the Office of Community Wealth Building and community organizations provide support, but access is not scalable or easy to navigate. Employers also face barriers hiring youth: uncertainty around labor rules and no streamlined way to connect with prepared candidates.

### Constraints
- Respect state youth labor laws and parental consent requirements
- Prioritize privacy protections for minors (do not store personal information about teens)
- Avoid adding significant workload for City staff
- Recognize transportation limitations affecting youth job access
- Complement existing workforce and community programs rather than replace them
- Do not make employment determinations or eligibility rulings

### Success would mean
- Youth have one clear place to explore employment opportunities and career experiences
- Teens receive guidance on preparing for their first job
- Required documents can be understood and organized with guidance (not stored by the tool)
- Employers feel more confident about what hiring youth requires
- More Richmond youth successfully access early employment opportunities

### Gaps (CRITICAL — D3=1)
No datasets linked. Missing:
- List of current youth employment programs (name, org, age eligibility, application window, locations)
- Employer directory willing to hire youth
- Virginia youth labor law summary in plain language
- GRTC transit routes overlaid with employer/program locations
- No workforce development section in Richmond open data catalog

Teams must curate this content manually or scope to guidance without live data.

## 2) Maternal Health Data Coordination

Score: 23/32 — Needs work

### Problem statement
How might we use technology to reduce fragmented and duplicative maternal health data analysis across agencies and nonprofit organizations so that Richmond's maternal health ecosystem can operate from unified, consistent metrics while respecting data-sharing limitations and staffing constraints?

### Context
Maternal health stakeholders across Richmond rely on multiple public datasets: Virginia Department of Health (VDH) maternal health data, American Community Survey (ACS) population data, Kids Count Virginia indicators, and school and insurance coverage data. Organizations independently compile and analyze these datasets, producing duplicated effort, inconsistent metrics, and fragmented reporting. Reduced federal funding and increasing pressure to demonstrate measurable outcomes make alignment around shared indicators urgent.

### Constraints
- Operate within legal and policy limits around sharing health data
- Work with incomplete or inconsistently available datasets
- Respect limited staffing capacity across agencies and nonprofits
- Remain cost-effective and sustainable
- Support — rather than replace — expert interpretation of health data
- Do not make clinical recommendations or patient-level health claims

### Success would mean
- Organizations no longer recreate the same datasets independently
- Maternal health indicators are defined and measured consistently
- Grant applications use shared and standardized data
- Staff time shifts from manual data compilation to strategic analysis
- Funding and interventions are directed toward evidence-based priorities

### Gaps
- URLs for four named data sources not yet linked: VDH maternal health portal, Census ACS tables, Kids Count Virginia, VDOE data
- Data format and access method for each source unspecified (API, CSV, web table?)
- Tools organizations currently use (Excel, Tableau, custom scripts?) undocumented
- Output format for a shared dashboard not defined (stakeholder alignment needed)
