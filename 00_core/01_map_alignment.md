# MAP Alignment - Thriving Families

## Richmond MAP (Master Action Plan) Connection

The Thriving Families pillar aligns to Richmond's MAP framework priorities around economic mobility, health equity, and family stability. The two problem statements in this pillar address distinct but related dimensions of family thriving.

## Youth Employment Alignment

**MAP theme:** Economic mobility and youth development
- Youth employment is a foundational step in the economic participation pipeline.
- Early work experience connects to long-term workforce attachment, which MAP prioritizes for underrepresented communities.
- The Office of Community Wealth Building's existing programs are referenced as partial infrastructure.
- Gaps in scalability and navigation are explicitly named in the problem statement.

**Who is affected:**
- Richmond youth ages 14–18, especially those without strong adult support networks
- Community organizations providing mentorship and workforce preparation
- Small employers willing to hire teens but uncertain about compliance

**Technology role:**
- Navigation and guidance (not employment placement or record-keeping)
- Making existing programs and pathways legible
- Reducing preparation friction so more youth can access opportunities

## Maternal Health Data Alignment

**MAP theme:** Health equity and evidence-based resource allocation
- Maternal health disparities in Richmond are significant; Black mothers face substantially higher rates of adverse outcomes.
- Fragmented and duplicated data analysis reduces the time stakeholders can spend on action.
- Aligned metrics would strengthen grant applications and advocacy efforts during a period of reduced federal funding.

**Who is affected:**
- Nonprofit researchers and health advocates
- City and state agency staff compiling reports
- Grant-makers and funders who need consistent indicators
- Ultimately, Richmond mothers and families who benefit from coordinated, evidence-based programs

**Technology role:**
- Aggregation and standardization of public data
- Shared dashboard or indicator set
- Reducing time spent on manual compilation so experts can focus on interpretation and action

## Relationship Between the Two Problems

Both problems involve:
- Fragmentation of information across organizations
- Duplicated effort that wastes limited capacity
- A population that would benefit from coordination but cannot force it
- Technology as a navigation and coordination layer, not a decision-making layer

Neither problem requires new legal authority, official system integration, or access to private data.
Both are weekend-tractable if teams scope to content curation and guided interfaces rather than live data pipelines.
