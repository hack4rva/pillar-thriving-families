# Pillar Overview - Thriving Families

## Purpose
This repository is a markdown knowledge base for teams working on the Thriving Families pillar in the Richmond Civic Hackathon. It helps teams understand the problem space, review problem statements, explore public data, and shape realistic weekend MVPs.

## Pillar framing
Thriving Families focuses on helping Richmond families and young people access opportunity, support, and coordinated services, including:
- youth workforce development and early employment access
- job readiness preparation for teens without strong adult support networks
- reducing employer friction in hiring youth
- reducing fragmented maternal health data analysis across agencies and nonprofits
- aligning shared indicators and metrics for health equity advocacy
- family economic stability as a foundation for wellbeing

## Why this matters
Early employment access and maternal health equity affect:
- long-term workforce attachment for young Richmonders
- confidence and skill-building in teens who lack adult networks
- evidence-based targeting of health interventions and funding
- organizational capacity freed from duplicated data work
- equity in health outcomes across Richmond neighborhoods

## Useful prototype directions
Prototypes that often help in this pillar:
- make public information easier to understand (labor law, work permits, health data)
- make resources easier to find and compare (programs, employers, health orgs)
- make public data easier to explore (VDH, ACS, Kids Count)
- help frontline organizations coordinate information instead of recreating it
- reveal actionable patterns for youth, employers, researchers, and staff

## Less useful directions
Prototypes that tend to struggle in a weekend:
- require policy changes or new legal authority (new youth employment programs)
- require integration with official City HR or health systems
- make employment eligibility or clinical determinations
- depend on private youth records (FERPA, COPPA) or confidential health data
- attempt to replace trained workforce counselors, social workers, or healthcare providers

## Both problems share a common constraint
Neither problem has a well-organized, verified public dataset ready to use.
Youth Employment (D3=1) is more severe: no compiled program list, no employer directory, no transit overlay.
Maternal Health (D3=3) is more tractable: four named public sources exist but URLs are unverified.
Teams must be honest about what they can curate in 48 hours versus what they assume exists.
