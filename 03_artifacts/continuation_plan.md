- City of Richmond, Office of Community Wealth Building — administers youth workforce programs; natural institutional home

### What a continuation partner would need from you
1. A working prototype with source-linked content
2. A clear explanation of how the tool complements (not replaces) their existing programs
3. An evidence log showing that the content is accurate and verified
4. A lightweight maintenance plan (who updates program listings and when)
5. Privacy documentation confirming the tool stores no personal information about minors

### 30-day next steps (post-hackathon)
- Contact one named champion and schedule a 30-minute demo
- Walk through the prototype and ask: "Does this accurately represent your program? What's missing?"
- Document one concrete improvement based on feedback
- Confirm whether the organization would be willing to host or maintain the tool

### 60-day next steps

- Add a plain-language Virginia work permit guide reviewed by someone with legal literacy
- Build a lightweight content update workflow (GitHub issue, form submission, or direct edit)

### 90-day next steps

- Determine whether a school-year version is feasible (internship/shadowing, not just summer)
- Explore whether OCWB would link to or host the tool on rva.gov

## Maternal Health — Continuation Paths

### Potential champions
- Richmond health nonprofits — any organization that participated in the rubric working session
- Virginia Department of Health (VDH) — data publisher; potential endorser of a reference tool
- Richmond City Health District — local public health authority
- Funders/foundations currently supporting maternal health work in Richmond

### What a continuation partner would need from you
1. A working dashboard or indicator dictionary with source citations
2. Demonstrated accuracy: show that your numbers match the official source
3. A data update plan: how will indicators be refreshed when VDH or Census releases new data?
4. A governance proposal: who decides which indicators to include or retire?
5. Evidence that the tool reduces — not adds — burden for staff

### 30-day next steps
- Share the prototype with 2–3 Richmond health researchers and gather structured feedback
- Identify one indicator that caused a discrepancy in real grant applications and validate your tool's handling of it
- Verify all data source URLs are stable and bookmarked

### 60-day next steps
- Expand indicator set to 12–15 based on user feedback
- Build automated data refresh for ACS (Census API is stable and free)
- Document the methodology for each indicator so a new user can understand and trust it

### 90-day next steps
- Explore hosting at a Richmond health org or academic institution (VCU, VCU Health, Partnership for Healthy Richmond)
- Identify a grant opportunity to fund continued development
- Propose the tool to Richmond's maternal health coalition (if one exists) as a shared resource
