# Benchmark Scan — National Comparables

This file documents national models relevant to both Thriving Families problem areas. Use these for inspiration, not as proof of Richmond applicability. Verify any cited features against the actual tools before claiming comparability.

---

## Youth Employment Tools

### YouthWorks Boston
- **What:** City of Boston's summer youth employment program with an online application portal
- **Relevance:** Structured online application for youth, with employer matching and program management
- **URL to verify:** https://www.boston.gov/departments/youth-employment-and-opportunity
- **Lesson for Richmond:** A centralized portal is achievable and valuable. Richmond lacks this. A weekend MVP could prototype the discovery layer without the full application backend.
- **Caution:** Boston has a dedicated City budget and staff for youth employment. Richmond context is different.

### NYC Summer Youth Employment Program (SYEP)
- **What:** One of the largest municipal youth employment programs in the US; web portal for applications
- **URL to verify:** https://www.nyc.gov/site/dycd/services/jobs-internships/summer-youth-employment-program-syep.page
- **Lesson for Richmond:** Discovery and preparation guidance can be separated from the formal application system. A Richmond MVP does not need to be an application portal — a preparation guide is sufficient.
- **Caution:** NYC scale and infrastructure far exceed what Richmond teams can replicate.

### Pathways to Prosperity (various cities)
- **What:** A national initiative connecting youth to career pathways through partnerships between schools, employers, and community organizations
- **URL to verify:** https://jff.org/pathways-to-prosperity-network/
- **Lesson for Richmond:** The model of "interest → exposure → preparation → employment" is well-validated. A tech tool can support the navigation layer.
- **Caution:** The program model requires institutional partnerships; a weekend tool can only prototype the navigation layer.

### Virginia Career Works
- **What:** Virginia's statewide workforce development network, including services for youth
- **URL to verify:** https://www.vacareerworks.com/
- **Lesson for Richmond:** State infrastructure exists but is primarily adult-oriented and not Richmond-specific. A local tool could surface Richmond-specific pathways that state tools obscure.

### Ready to Work / CareerOneStop Youth Tools
- **What:** Federal DOL-funded resources for youth job readiness
- **URL to verify:** https://www.careeronestop.org/
- **Lesson for Richmond:** Generic national tools exist. Richmond teens need something that reflects local programs, local transit, and local employers — not national job boards.

## Maternal Health Data Tools

### March of Dimes Peristats
- **What:** National maternal and infant health statistics database by state and county
- **URL to verify:** https://www.marchofdimes.org/peristats
- **Lesson for Richmond:** A focused, well-labeled indicator set with clear sourcing is more useful than raw data. This is the design pattern to emulate.
- **Caution:** National data; does not replace VDH Richmond-specific data.

### County Health Rankings (Robert Wood Johnson Foundation)
- **What:** Annual rankings of county health outcomes and factors across all US counties
- **URL to verify:** https://www.countyhealthrankings.org/
- **Lesson for Richmond:** Clear indicator definitions, consistent methodology, and easy comparison are features users value. Richmond area is included.
- **Caution:** Indicators are broad health measures; maternal-specific depth requires VDH.

### Virginia Department of Health Community Health Profiles
- **What:** VDH's own community health data portal
- **URL to verify:** https://www.vdh.virginia.gov/data/
- **Lesson for Richmond:** The official source exists. The problem is not data absence — it is that the format, update cadence, and presentation require expert knowledge to use efficiently.

### WONDER (CDC)
- **What:** CDC's public health data query tool; includes maternal and infant mortality data
- **URL to verify:** https://wonder.cdc.gov/
- **Lesson for Richmond:** National data at the county level is available. Richmond teams can use this as a supplementary source to contextualize VDH data.

### HealthData.gov
- **What:** Federal health data portal with links to multiple health datasets
- **URL to verify:** https://healthdata.gov/
- **Lesson for Richmond:** Many public health datasets are available; the challenge is curation and normalization, not access.

## Prior Art Gaps

- A mobile-first, plain-language Virginia work permit guide for teens
- A Richmond-specific program directory for youth employment with real-time availability
- A shared indicator dashboard used across Richmond maternal health organizations
- A standard indicator dictionary agreed upon by Richmond health stakeholders
