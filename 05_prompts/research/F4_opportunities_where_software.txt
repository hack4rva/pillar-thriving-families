Opportunities F4 — Where Software Actually Helps vs. Where It Does Not

Analyze the Thriving Families problem areas and identify precisely where software can make a meaningful difference versus where the problem is primarily one of policy, funding, capacity, or human relationships that software cannot fix.

For youth employment: where does software help (navigation, preparation guidance, information aggregation) and where does it fail (transportation access, adult mentorship, employer relationships, work permit enforcement)?

For maternal health data: where does software help (data aggregation, indicator standardization, shared access) and where does it fail (organizational incentives to share, expert interpretation, funding decisions, clinical interventions)?

Be honest about the limits of weekend-hackathon software in addressing systemic problems.

Output
- Matrix: problem dimension × software helpfulness (high / medium / low / counterproductive)
- Rationale for each assessment
- Inferences (clearly labeled)
- Unknowns
