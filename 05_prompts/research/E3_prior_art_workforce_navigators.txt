Prior Art E3 — Workforce Pathway Navigator Tools

Research the landscape of workforce pathway navigator tools — tools that help users (especially inexperienced users) understand multi-step processes for entering employment or a career pathway. Include examples from youth-focused tools, adult workforce tools, and civic-tech projects.

For each tool: describe the navigation approach (wizard, checklist, decision tree, chatbot), the content model (curated vs. live data), what the user outcome is, and whether the tool has been evaluated for effectiveness.

Assess: what makes a pathway navigator usable by someone with no prior knowledge of the system, what the minimum viable content set is for a youth employment navigator, and how tools handle the problem of stale information.

Output
- Tool inventory with navigation approach and content model
- Lessons for a Richmond youth employment navigator
- Minimum viable content set (what you absolutely need for a credible demo)
- Inferences (clearly labeled)
- Unknowns
