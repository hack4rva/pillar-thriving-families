User & Stakeholder B5 — Digital Equity: Teen Access to Technology in Richmond

Investigate the digital equity landscape for Richmond teens ages 14–18. Identify: what share of teens have reliable smartphone access, what share have home internet access, what disparities exist by neighborhood or income, and what barriers exist to using web-based tools for job searching and application.

Also investigate: language accessibility needs for Richmond teen populations (Spanish, other languages), and what low-bandwidth or offline-friendly approaches exist for youth-facing tools.

Use only verifiable public sources (Census, ACS, FCC, school district data, nonprofit research). Clearly label inferences.

This research informs accessibility decisions for an MVP targeting Richmond youth employment access.

Output
- Facts on device and internet access for Richmond teens (with source titles)
- Disparities by income/neighborhood (if documented)
- Language access considerations
- Implications for MVP design choices (mobile-first, offline fallback, etc.)
- Inferences (clearly labeled)
- Unknowns
