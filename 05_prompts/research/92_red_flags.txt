Cross-Cutting — Red Flags and Risk Inventory

Identify the most serious risks and red flags for civic-tech teams working on the Thriving Families pillar in Richmond. Cover both youth employment and maternal health data coordination.

Critical risk categories to cover:
1. Minor privacy and data protection (COPPA, FERPA implications for tools serving teens under 18)
2. Unsafe youth employment records (risk of creating unofficial records that could be misused)
3. Health claims (harm from incorrect or misleading maternal health guidance)
4. Employer-teen matching (legal and safety risks if the tool is perceived as facilitating employment contracts)
5. Data staleness (risk of displaying outdated program availability or health statistics)
6. Digital equity (risk of excluding youth without smartphones or reliable internet)
7. Overclaiming (risk of presenting the tool as authoritative without validation)

Output
- Flagged risk per category with severity (high / medium / low)
- Specific guardrail language to add to the tool
- Inferences (clearly labeled)
- Unknowns
