Master Research Prompt — Thriving Families Pillar

Conduct a comprehensive research pass on the two problem areas in the Richmond Civic Hackathon Thriving Families pillar: (1) youth employment pathways for Richmond teens ages 14–18, and (2) maternal health data coordination across Richmond agencies and nonprofits.

For youth employment: identify the current landscape of programs, barriers, and technology tools in Richmond and comparable cities. Focus on the gap between teen interest and successful first employment — including the preparation friction (documents, work permits, labor law compliance, transportation) and the discovery friction (which programs exist, who qualifies, how to apply).

For maternal health data: identify the current fragmentation of data analysis, the named public sources (VDH, ACS, Kids Count Virginia, VDOE), what coordination tools exist nationally, and what Richmond-specific disparities and stakeholders are documented.

For both: identify what data is publicly available, what is missing, what tools exist nationally, what has been tried in Richmond, and where technology can realistically help in a 48-hour hackathon.

Output
- Facts (with source titles)
- Inferences (clearly labeled)
- Unknowns (what cannot be verified publicly)
- Top 3 research questions that remain open after this pass
- Top 3 MVP opportunities supported by evidence
