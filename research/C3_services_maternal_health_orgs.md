
# C3 Services – Maternal Health Organizations

*Document updated 2026-03-24* 

## Executive Summary

This document outlines the strategic partnerships and complementary services between Community Care Cooperative (C3) and leading global and national maternal health organizations. By mapping C3's community-engagement and data-driven care coordination models against the specialized programs of these entities, stakeholders can identify referral pathways, funding alignments, and policy advocacy opportunities. Key highlights include C3's alignment with WHO and UNFPA data standards, direct referral mechanisms with specialized care providers, and joint capacity-building initiatives to reduce maternal mortality and morbidity.

---

## 1. Introduction

Community Care Cooperative (C3) is a national non-profit Accountable Care Organization that partners with health centers and social-service agencies to improve maternal health outcomes [1] [2]. The "C3 Services – Maternal Health Orgs" document lists the key external partners whose programs complement C3’s work, provides a brief description of each organization, and links to their authoritative web pages.

## 3. Partner Organizations

### 3.1 World Health Organization (WHO) – Maternal Health

* **Founded:** 1948 
* **Mission:** Promote health, keep the world safe, and serve the vulnerable. 
* **Maternal-Health Focus:** Global standards, quality-of-care guidelines, and data tracking for maternal mortality [4]. 
* **Key Assets:** WHO Maternal-Health Fact Sheets, "Every Newborn Action Plan (ENAP)" initiative. 
* **Website:** <https://www.who.int/health-topics/maternal-health> [4]

---

### 3.2 United Nations Population Fund (UNFPA)

* **Founded:** 1967 
* **Mission:** Deliver a world where every pregnancy is wanted, every childbirth is safe, and every young person’s potential is fulfilled [5]. 
* **Maternal-Health Focus:** Family-planning services, skilled birth attendance, obstetric fistula prevention, maternal-death surveillance [5]. 
* **Website:** <https://www.unfpa.org/maternal-health> [5]

---

### 3.3 United Nations Children’s Fund (UNICEF) – Maternal & Newborn Health

* **Founded:** 1946 
* **Mission:** Advocate for the protection of children's rights, to help meet their basic needs and to expand their opportunities to reach their full potential. 
* **Maternal-Health Focus:** Integrated maternal-newborn packages, community health-worker outreach, emergency obstetric care, kangaroo-care [6]. 
* **Website:** <https://www.unicef.org/health/maternal-and-newborn-health> [6]

---

### 3.4 Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) – Maternal Mortality Prevention

* **Founded:** 1946 
* **Mission:** Works 24/7 to protect America from health, safety and security threats, both foreign and in the U.S. 
* **Maternal-Health Focus:** Surveillance of pregnancy-related deaths, state-level perinatal quality collaboratives, evidence-based guidelines [7]. 
* **Website:** <https://www.cdc.gov/maternal-mortality/index.html> [7]

---

### 3.5 American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists (ACOG) – Women’s Health

* **Founded:** 1951 
* **Mission:** Support our members to improve the lives of all people seeking obstetric and gynecologic care, their families, and communities. 
* **Maternal-Health Services:** Clinical practice guidelines, provider education, advocacy for equitable maternity care. 
* **Website:** <https://www.acog.org/clinical> [8]

* **Website:** <https://www.marchofdimes.org/our-work/programs> [9]

## 5. How C3 Leverages These Partnerships

1. **Data Alignment** – C3’s assessment platforms map community-level data to the WHO and UNFPA maternal-health indicators, enabling real-time gaps analysis [3] [4] [5]. 
2. **Referral Engine** – Through formal partnerships with social services organizations across Massachusetts, C3 ports patients directly to specialized surgical or social-service slots [3]. 
3. **Policy Advocacy** – C3 collaborates with organizations like ACOG, FIGO, and the White Ribbon Alliance to push policies that fund Medicaid extensions and improve respectful maternity care [8] [11] [13]. 
4. **Capacity Building** – Joint training and alignment with NICHD and CDC guidelines keep C3 clinicians up-to-date on evidence-based protocols, such as the IMPROVE initiative's recommended pathways [7] [22].
