# Richmond Teens to Paychecks: Fast, Software-Shaped Wins by Sunday

## Executive Summary

General job boards list hundreds of "teen" roles with starting pay between $12.77 and $18.00 per hour, but critical details like age minimums and shift rules are buried [1] [2] [3] [4] [5]. ## Legal and Process Frictions
Virginia's rules create a hard gate for 14- and 15-year-olds and strict scheduling constraints for all minors. Solving this guidance gap reduces applicant drop-offs.

### Certification Pipeline: Offer-First Rule and 24-Hour Review
In Virginia, 14- and 15-year-olds are required to obtain an Employment Certificate, but they must not begin the registration process until they have a firm offer of employment [6]. The application is a tri-party process requiring input from the youth, the employer, and a parent/guardian [6]. Once submitted through the Virginia Electronic Employment Certificate System, the Department of Labor and Industry (DOLI) generally reviews it within 24 hours [6]. Manual forms are also available but require notarization by the parent/guardian [6] [11].

### Hours and Prohibited Work: What Employers Fear
Employers face strict regulations regarding the hours minors can work and the tasks they can perform. For 14- and 15-year-olds, work is limited to 3 hours on school days and 18 hours per week during school weeks, with prohibitions on working before 7:00 AM or after 7:00 PM [9]. Violations of child labor laws can result in federal fines of up to $10,000 per violation, criminal prosecution, and reputational damage [9].

## Opportunity Design
We have identified three software-shaped wins aligned to Discovery, Preparation, and Employer Friction that can be stood up by Sunday and tested with real teens.

### 1. Discovery: RVA Teen Jobs + Transit Microdirectory
* **User:** 16-17 year olds seeking immediate employment.
* **Pain:** Filtering out 18+ noise on major job boards and figuring out if they can actually commute to the job.
* **Content/Data:** Curated listings from Indeed, Glassdoor, and Snagajob, combined with GRTC route notes and last-bus times [8] [1] [3] [4].
* **Implementation Approach:** A mobile-optimized web list or SMS bot featuring 30-50 manually curated roles. Listings will include tags like "16+," "weekend," and "no experience," alongside explicit pay rates and transit cues (e.g., "Route 1: every 15 min until 11pm; stop 0.2 mi").
* **Demo Success Criteria:** 30-50 live, teen-eligible Richmond job cards; 10+ clicks/interactions from test teens during the Sunday demo.

### 2. Preparation: 14-15 "First Job" Permit Kit
* **User:** 14-15 year olds and their parents.
* **Pain:** The catch-22 of needing a firm job offer before applying for a work permit, combined with the friction of state portals and notarization requirements [6] [11].
* **Content/Data:** DOLI portal links, manual form PDFs, hours rules summaries, and Child Labor unit contact info [6] [9] [12] [11].
* **Implementation Approach:** A one-page digital checklist and "Starter Kit." It will include five employer-ready scripts to help teens secure the initial offer, parent-lite guidance, and direct links to the DOLI VAeECS system.
* **Demo Success Criteria:** A published one-page kit with working links; 3+ teens successfully complete step 1 (youth registration) in the DOLI portal (without final submission) during testing.

### 3. Employer Friction: "Hire a Teen in RVA" One-Pager + Pledge
* **User:** Local Richmond business owners and hiring managers.
* **Pain:** Fear of compliance violations, $10,000 fines, and the administrative burden of tracking minor hours and permits [9].
* **Content/Data:** DOLI employer registration steps, hour restrictions (school vs. non-school weeks), record-keeping requirements, and a contact email for quick questions [6] [9].
* **Implementation Approach:** A downloadable PDF one-pager demystifying the rules, paired with a simple Google Form where employers can ask questions or pledge to list teen-friendly roles.
* **Demo Success Criteria:** Publication of the one-pager; collection of 3-5 employer pledges or inquiries via the form by Sunday.

## Risks, Edge Cases, and Mitigations
* **Age Mislabeling on Postings:** Job boards often obscure age minimums. *Mitigation:* Only include listings with explicit minimum ages or those verified by phone. Label unknowns clearly as "verify age."
* **Late Shifts and School-Night Restrictions:** Minors cannot work late on school nights [9]. *Mitigation:* Add an "hours caution" badge for roles with late closings and link directly to VA hours rules.
* **Parent Consent Friction for 14-15:** Manual forms require notarization [11]. *Mitigation:* Provide an SMS-ready parent note template and a clear notary reminder if they opt out of the electronic portal.
* **Program Timing:** YES deadlines fluctuate. *Mitigation:* Display application status and "last updated" dates prominently [7].
* **Richmond CA Confusion:** *Mitigation:* Apply prominent "Richmond, VA" tags across all interfaces and strictly exclude CA content [7] [20].
